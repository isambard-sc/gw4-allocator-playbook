# Managing Awards on the [Allocate Portal](https://allocate.isambard.ac.uk)

## Creating an Award

1. Log into the [Allocate Portal](https://allocate.isambard.ac.uk) using your account.
2. Navigate to the **Organisation** page for the appropriate institution (Cardiff University, University of Bath, or University of Exeter).
3. Click on the **Awards** tab.
4. Click the **Add** button to create a new Award.
5. In the pop-up, fill in the details:
   - **Award Name**
   - **Award End Date** (calculated from the duration from today)
   - **Award Description** (if available)
6. The project short name must be unique. Use the format `university-something`, where `university` is `bath`, `cardiff`, or `exeter`, and `something` is a short identifier for the project based on the project title — e.g., `bath-ai-research`.
7. Click **Create**. Waldur will automatically take you to the award dashboard once it is created.
8. From the award dashboard, go to the **Resources** tab. Click **Add** to add a new resource. Choose **Isambard 3** from the appropriate **Allocatable Offerings** section (i.e. "Cardiff Allocatable Offerings", "Bath Allocatable Offerings", or "Exeter Allocatable Offerings"). This opens the Offering page — click **Create** to create the offering with default settings. It will take 10–30 seconds to create.
9. Once created, click **Options** in the middle/top menu to see the resource options. You should see the **Allocation** with a default value of 1000 node hours. Click the **Edit** button on the right to open the dialog and change the allocation to match the size of the award in the spreadsheet. Click **Save**.

You have now added all necessary information except the PI details.

## Adding the PI

Go to the award dashboard and verify the details look correct (award name, description, and node hours allocation).

1. Click on the **Team** tab, then click **Add**, then **Member**.
2. Click on the **User** field. If the user already exists on the system, typing their email will list their account for selection (toggle **Show users outside organization** if needed). Otherwise, click **Create user** and enter their email, first name, and last name.
3. Click on the **Role** field and select **Project Lead** from the drop-down.
4. Click **Add role**.

The award is now created and the PI has been added. This information will be communicated to the Bristol Portal, where it must be approved before the project is onboarded. Once onboarded, the Project Lead will receive an invitation to join the project.

> **Note:** A member of the BriCS team reviews awards daily for approval, but please email the team when you create a project to ensure it is not missed.

## Adding Top-Ups or Extensions

### Extending the End Date

1. Go to the award dashboard in the [Allocate Portal](https://allocate.isambard.ac.uk).
2. Click **Edit** at the top to edit the award details.
3. Click the **Edit** button to the right of the **End Date** field. Choose the new end date and click **Save**. The change will be communicated automatically to the Bristol Portal, which will update the project there too.

### Adding Additional Node Hours

1. Go to the award page in the [Allocate Portal](https://allocate.isambard.ac.uk).
2. Click on the **Resources** tab.
3. Click on **Isambard 3** to go to the resource page.
4. Click **Options** in the middle/top menu to see the resource options. You should see the **Allocation** with the current allocation value.
5. Click the **Edit** button on the right. Change the allocation to the **total new allocation** (i.e. current value plus the top-up, or the absolute value if the institution has specified the new total). Click **Save**. This will automatically send an update request to the Bristol Portal.

> **Note:** These requests need to be approved in the Bristol Portal.
>
> **Warning:** The allocation update can sometimes fail for older projects or projects with previous allocation issues. Please email the BriCS team after adding an extension so that any issues can be identified and resolved promptly.
